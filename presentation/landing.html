Urban AI for Heat-Resilient European Cities
How strategic de-sealing outperforms tree planting for urban cooling

Gerardo Ezequiel Martin Carreno
MSc Urban Spatial Science — UCL CASA

View Presentation GitHub Repository

Abstract

European cities face escalating heat risk, yet struggle to translate abundant urban data into actionable cooling strategies. This research develops an integrated analytical framework that predicts urban heat intensity across six European cities — Amsterdam, Athens, Barcelona, Berlin, Madrid, and Paris — using 118 features from five complementary data sources at 30-metre resolution.

Using XGBoost with hierarchical climate-zone blending (R² = 0.841) and SHAP-based interpretability, the framework reveals that surface permeability matters approximately three times more than vegetation for temperature reduction — challenging the prevailing emphasis on tree planting. A regime boundary at 50% de-sealing enables evaporative cooling pathways that achieve 95% of maximum impact with 60% fewer resources.

The framework generates city-specific priority maps combining heat risk, demographic vulnerability, and cooling potential to guide equitable intervention targeting, while acknowledging the risk of green gentrification.
